Pallet Detection

Updated 03.07.2024

Pallet detection requirements

Detecting the pallet -- field of view:

At least one of the legs of the pallet is assumed to be in front of the laser scanner within a field of view of 30 degrees

The algorithm assumes the pallet will be located directly in front of the laser scanner.

Field of view explained.

Detecting the pallet -- maximum error:

  • The angle of the pallet relative to the laser scanner must be less than 20 degrees.

  • The maximum error for the pallet's nominal location is about +-15 cm (depending on the pallet dimensions).

    • In good circumstances the pallet can be correctly detected even if the displacement is larger, but it can't be always guaranteed.

  • The space between the pallets next to each other should differ from the distance between the legs of the pallet.

Distance between pallets.

Detecting from stringer board

  • The pallet can be also detected based on the top part of a pallet called stringer board. If the pallet is detected from the stringer board, the pallets must be separated from each other at least by 10 cm.

Using pallet detection

Pallet detection can be used to drive the AGV accurately under a pallet, even if the pallet is not perfectly on the location defined as a symbolic point in Navithor Tools. Pallet detection detects the target and corrects the AGVs driving according to it.

Using pallet detection requires Navitrol license which includes the feature, otherwise Navitrol will stop in an error, if the pallet detection is activated without the proper license.

Pallet detection can be used in combination with 'Load' or 'Load, Unload' symbolic point. If pallet detection is enabled for such a location and the AGV is heading towards the location, Navitrol activates pallet detection and tries to find the target. If the target is found and certain restrictions are met, the AGV starts a docking procedure and corrects the trajectory to drive precisely under the pallet. For more information on docking, see Docking.

Pallet detection can be enabled with a quick selection in Navithor Tools under the Symbolic Points tool -> Edit Symbolic Points -> 'Enable pallet detection':

Enabling pallet detection in Navithor Tools

If pallet detection is not enabled with this checkbox, AGV will drive to the location blindly.

There are several warnings, that can show up, if something goes wrong with pallet detection. If detecting the target fails, Navitrol will show up target detection error. If pallet is found, but the restrictions for starting the docking are not met, Navitrol shows instead a target approach error. If docking is started, but the error is too big to correct when AGV is closer to the pallet, Navitrol will show up the same warning. More detailed explanation about these errors are given in Chapter 8.2. The exact warnings are following:

612, WARNING_TARGET_NOT_DETECTED, "Target for docking not detected"

613, WARNING_TARGET_APPROACH_ERROR, "Target location differs too much from expected"

Some parameters, which are discussed later in this document, need to be also changed in Navitrol to activate pallet detection.

Mounting laser scanner for pallet detection

Scanner angle related to the floor is important to consider when the scanner is mounted to the AGV. In ideal case, the scanner can see the legs of the pallet from the distance and also on close range.

Calibrating laser scanner for pallet detection

NOTE! If the pallet detection scanner is used only for detecting pallets and not for localization, this method should be used to calibrate the scanner. If it is used also on localization, then it should be calibrated in normal way.

The angle of the pallet detection scanner (scan_rear_sen_ang or scan_front_sen_ang depending which scanner is used) plays a key role in the pallet detection accuracy. A clear sign of wrong scanner angle is a sidewise drifting pallet position during the approaching drive.

A good method to calibrate the scanner is to do it in a controlled pallet detection drive as follows:

  • Plan a straight route of approximately 4 meters in an environment with good positioning confidence. Mark a symbolic point with pallet detection tag at the end of the route.

  • Set the pallet detection parameters so that the pallet detection is active during the entire route (target_detection_type_selection = 3, target_detection_always_enabled = 1) and disable the docking to keep the AGV on the route (docking_to_target_enabled = 0).

  • Place a pallet accurately on the AGV fork and give the AGV a drive order to the symbolic point so that it unloads the pallet accurately at the symbolic point location. The idea is just to have the pallet on the symbolic point.

  • Give the AGV a drive order to the other end of the route.

  • Give the AGV a drive order to the symbolic point where the pallet is and verify that the pallet detection is active during the drive.

  • When the drive is ready, disconnect the Monitor from the navigation so that the drive can be analyzed by sliding back and forth with the progress bar (see below)

Monitor Progress bar.

  • Slide backwards until the first pallet detection visualizations in the beginning of the drive (green pallet square on the map) and freeze the view as shown in the figure below.

Frozen initial pallet position at start of drive.

  • Slide forwards again until the AGV reaches the pallet and monitor how the pallet position is drifting while the AGV approaches the pallet.

  • If the pallet position is constantly drifting sideways in one direction, this is caused by an error in the scanner angle, see the second figure below.

  • Adjust the scanner angle in the parameter file, unfreeze the Monitor view, reconnect the Monitor and request Navitrol to update the parameters.

  • Repeat the pallet detection drive, analyzing the pallet drift during the drive and adjusting the scanner angle until the drift is small and randomly to both sides of the route.

  • Now the scanner angle should be accurately calibrated.

Frozen pallet position and current pallet position mismatch.

Required Navitrol parameters

Following parameters have to be always set to use pallet detection:

I,target_detection_type_selection, 3

# Enables reflector-based docking or pallet detection, 0 = disabled, 1 = reflector docking, 2 = pallet detection, 3 = pallet detection v2, 4 = external target detection, 5 = supervisor controlled;

NOTE! There are two pallet detection versions, but version 2 (target_detection_type_selection = 3) should be used from now on. The first version of pallet detection (parameter value 2) should not be used anymore. Navitec Systems have not updated this version anymore since 2018.

Main difference is that version 2 supports using more than 1 scanner for pallet detection, can detect the pallet in any driving direction and works with all omni headings.

I,target_detection_scan_selection, 1 or 2

# Defines which scanners are used for target detection: 1 = rear scanner only, 2 = front scanner only, 3 = both scanners (supported only in reflector-based detection!), 4 = use scanner in driving direction, 5 = alternate between scanners

NOTE! More than two scanners are supported with target_detection_multi_scan_selection parameter. target_detection_scan_selection has to be 0 to activate that parameter.

I,docking_to_target_enabled, 1

# 1 = Enables docking to target. 2 = docking with custom route. If target_detection_type_selection == 0, won't do anything

F,docking_max_start_distance, 3.5 # Beyond maximum distance docking won't be triggered

Differences between these two docking types are explained in Chapters 8.4 and 8.5.

Pallet models used for the detection has to be defined as Navitrol parameters. They are array parameters and maximum number of models used is 5. The algorithm tries to detect the best match from the different models given. At least one models has to be always defined.

An example of EUR-pallet in Navitrol parameters:

ParameterValue
I,pallet_model_enabled[0],1
S,pallet_model_description[0],"EUR-pallet"
F,pallet_model_width_y[0],0.800
F,pallet_model_length_x[0],1.200
I,pallet_model_n_legs_x[0],3
I,pallet_model_n_legs_y[0],3
F,pallet_model_mid_leg_width_y[0],0.145
F,pallet_model_mid_leg_length_x[0],0.145
F,pallet_model_side_leg_width_y[0],0.100
F,pallet_model_side_leg_length_x[0],0.145

The second model can be given by changing the array index to [1], the third model with [2] and so on. Individual models can be temporarily disabled by changing the pallet_model_enabled to 0, so you don't need to remove the whole model from parameter file to disable it. At least one model has to be enabled all the time.

NOTE! Pallet detection algorithm tries to find the best fitting model from these, but if the models are too similar to each other, false positives can happen.

  • Larger differences between pallet models is always better.

  • Additional models should be avoided, if not necessary

Scanner modes

The target_detection_scan_selection parameter has different options for the scanner use. This parameter is only meant to define what scanner data is used for pallet detection. The scanner has to be enabled with this parameter: scan_rear_front_selection.

  • Mode 1: Uses scanner that is defined with scanner_model_rear parameter.

  • Mode 2: Uses scanner that is defined with scanner_model_front parameter.

  • Mode 3: Uses data from both front and rear scanners. Not supported with target_detection_type_selection = 2, the scanner mode 4 should be used instead.

  • Mode 4: Uses data from scanner based on the driving direction. When driving forward, using front scanner, when driving backwards use the rear scanner. Uses wheel speed to define the driving direction (positive forwards, negative backwards). For example: Steer wheel for steer drive, fastest wheel of diff drive. If the AGV is standing still, using the last scanner.

The target_detection_multi_scan_selection parameter supports more scanners. If the target_detection_scan_selection is 0, this parameter is used instead. This parameter is array parameter and each array index matches to the scanner:

target_detection_multi_scan_selection[0] = Rear scanner

target_detection_multi_scan_selection[1] = Front scanner

target_detection_multi_scan_selection[2] = Measurement 0 scanner

target_detection_multi_scan_selection[3] = Measurement 1 scanner

Then the parameter values define what mode is used.

  • Mode 1: Uses this scanner only. For example, target_detection_multi_scan_selection[2] = 1 uses only measurement 0 scanner for target detection.

  • Mode 2: Uses all defined scanners. For example, target_detection_multi_scan_selection[2] = 2 and target_detection_multi_scan_selection[3] = 2 both measurement 0 and measurement 1 scanners are used for target detection.

  • Mode 3: Alternate primary scanner. The idea is that target detection uses only data from the received scanner (primary or secondary). Mainly used on pattern detection (target_detection_type_selection = 1 and refl_det_version = 2).

  • Mode 4: Alternate secondary scanner.

  • Mode 5: Use this scanner when driving forwards. For example, target_detection_multi_scan_selection[1] = 5. Front scanner is used only when AGV is driving forwards.

  • Mode 6: Use this scanner when driving backwards. For example, target_detection_multi_scan_selection[1] = 6. Front scanner is used only when AGV is driving backwards.

  • Mode 7: Use this scanner when driving sideways to the right (should be used only with omni).

  • Mode 8: Use this scanner when driving sideways to the left (should be used only with omni).

Pallet detection

Search area

The search area is an area that defines what scanner points are included in pallet detection algorithm.

  • For non-buffer lane symbolic points with load detection enabled, the search area is defined first as a circle and then limited based on the pallet width we are searching for (see two figures below). The circle radius comes from pallet model dimension and pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add parameter. If there are multiple pallet models defined, then the pallet model that has the largest dimension is used.

Circle shaped search area around the symbolic point, marked as dashed blue line.

  • For buffer lanes, the circle is not used, but the search area is defined initially based on the first pallet model width defined on params.txt file and the pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add parameter as on the figure below. If there are multiple pallet models, then the search area is first limited based on the first model width and later limited based on the model we are searching for.

Search area based on pallet width. The area between the blue dashed lines is defined from route to the pallet width + pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add.

Start point for clustering

The start point for clustering is usually the closest point in AGV coordinate frame. If this point is not found from the closest legs of the pallet, the detection cannot find the pallet. There are two values that are used to select the start point:

  • Distance from scanner point to AGV navigation pose
  • Scanner point angle relative to the symbolic point. This angle is a difference between line that goes from AGV navigation pose to scanner point, and line that goes from AGV navigation pose to symbolic point

Parameter pallet_det_first_leg_ang_weight can be used to define the weight between these two values. The bigger the parameter value, the more important is the small angle. The smaller the value, the more important is the small distance.

Parameter pallet_det_use_dynamic_search_ang defines how the maximum angle for the start point is calculated.

  • If pallet_det_use_dynamic_search_ang is 1, then the angle is calculated with distance from AGV to the symbolic point + docking_stop_distance and the half pallet width + pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add. The idea is that the angle is little bit higher than the closest corner of the pallet. See figure below.

Maximum angle for the start point, illustrated as red line. The angle changes dynamically based on the AGV distance to the symbolic point. 1. docking_stop_distance parameter, 2. Pallet half width, 3. pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add parameter, 4. Maximum angle, 5. Distance from AGV to symbolic point.

  • If pallet_det_use_dynamic_search_ang is 0, then pallet_det_limit_initial_search_ang is used to define the max angle. See figure below.

Maximum angle for the start point, illustrated as red line. The angle is static and it does not change based on distance to the symbolic point. 1. Angle is defined only with pallet_det_limit_initial_search_ang parameter.

Linear model

Linear model is a detection feature that matches straight line for the pallet width. The idea is to detect the stringer board of the pallet or the load on the pallet if it has the width of the pallet.

Linear model can be enabled with parameter pallet_det_linear_models_enabled.

The parameter pallet_det_lin_mod_point_threshold define what is the maximum distance between consecutive points to create the straight line.

The parameter pallet_det_linear_model_max_err is maximum error between the model width (pallet_model_width_y paramater) and the detected line.

Example of the linear model. The pallet is detected based on the line width.

Asymmetric pallets

An asymmetric pallet is a pallet that is shaped like an isosceles trapezoid, meaning it is symmetric along one axis and asymmetric along the other. Each pallet is assumed to have four legs, visualized below:

Dimensions of an asymmetric pallet

An asymmetric pallet will create four models, from each possible direction of detection. The models are obtained as 0, 90, 180 and 270 degree rotations of the pallet around its axis.

Models obtained from an asymmetric pallet

The pose estimate (x, y, h) of an asymmetric pallet is obtained as the centre of the facade for the symmetric side or in line with the pallet leg that is nominally closer to the AGV. The side leg angle offset Y is used to compensate the computed heading, visualized in the Figure above. The position of the pallet is visualized as a blue circle in the below Figures


Pose estimation of an asymmetric pallet

For asymmetric pallets a heading distinction of 180 degrees must be made. This is done by the sign of the side leg angle offset Y weighted by parameter pallet_det_fitness_weight[7]. Asymmetric pallet detection logging has prefix Pallet detection: asymmetric that is visible, when parameter log_pallet_detection is set to 1.

Locking the target

The target is locked (meaning its position is not updated anymore) after the distance to target is close enough.

  • If target_detection_type_selection = 2, the distance parameter is pallet_det_min_dist_from_scanner.

  • If target_detection_type_selection = 3, the distance parameter is pallet_det_2_min_dist_from_nav_point_to_traj_end.

There is also model-based parameter pallet_model_min_dist_from_nav_point_to_traj_end[model index] that is used if the target should be locked in different distance for different pallet models.

Locking the model

This can be used if there are different pallet models. If the target is found multiple times in a row the algorithm can be locked to search only that specific model by setting the parameter pallet_det_2_lock_model_if_found_number_of_times to some reasonable value (e.g. 10 times). However, it is important to check if the old target is reset between different symbolic points. This can be tested by driving between two pallet detection points that has different pallet models and see which model is locked. If the last pallet model seems to be detected also on the next location, it indicates that the model is not reset between those locations. The old result is reset after there is no detection in number of times set by this parameter: target_max_n_times_missing (e.g. start with 100 times missing and decrease if still detect the model from the last symbolic point).

Wall check

Wall check example shows where the holes and walls are checked.

ParameterValuesDescription
I,pallet_det_wall_check[0],0to 2
I,pallet_det_wall_check[1],0 or 1
I,pallet_det_wall_check_points,3How many points at maximum from the leg are checked in the wall checks, before a decision is made that if there is empty space or not
F,pallet_det_wall_point_dist_threshold,0.01, 0.05, 0.20Maximum distance (meters) between points, that they are estimated to be linked together in the wall checks

There are three modes on the wall check:

Mode 1: pallet_det_wall_check[0] = 1

Check that there is empty space between the legs. If the space is not empty, solution is discarded.

Mode 2: pallet_det_wall_check[0] = 2

Check if there is a wall next to the legs. If found wall, discard the solution.

Mode 3: pallet_det_wall_check[1] = 1

Additional check that if there are pallets next to each other and the pallet detected is actually bigger model than should be (outer legs detected instead of the inner ones)

Example of wall check. The points circled with red are considered as leg and blue ones are included to the wall check. Here the pallet_det_wall_check_points is 6 and it means that if there are 6 scanner points next to the leg, the wall check will discard that result. The pallet_det_wall_point_dist_threshold is 0.05 so the purple point is too far from the blue points, so it is not included and then the blue ones below doesn't have enough points to be considered as wall.

Search only front legs

If the rear legs are not visible with the scanner or if they don't match with the pallet model parameters (e.g. the leg shape is not rectangle), it is possible to use only front legs for the detection. There might be also something else on the rear that doesn't give any reasonable results for the algorithm. For example, on the figure down below, the rear legs are different size than the front legs so it does not make sense to try to detect the rear legs.

Example of poor pallet detection scanner data on the rear legs.

If pallet_det_2_detect_only_front_legs is set to 1, the algorithm detects only front legs.

Object detection

Object detection is a sub feature of pallet detection in which instead of a pallet any group of scanner points that are close to each other and within the search area can be detected as the target. If several groups are found, the closest one is selected as the target.

The downsides of the object detection is that it can detect anything on the route, so the accuracy might vary and that it does not provide the angle of the target. The downsides makes object detection only compatible with docking methods, that do not use the target angle, such as docking with only distance control.

NameTypeRequired valueDescription
pallet_det_object_det_enabledInt1Enables object detection so that instead of pallets any cluster of scanner points inside the search area is detected as a target. target_detection_type_selection must have value 3 for this parameter to have an effect.

As a sub feature of pallet detection many of the parameters affecting pallet detection affect object detection as well with some differences. For example, pallet_model_width_y[0] is used to visualize the width of the found target.

Related parameters that can be adjusted:

  • target_detection_min_search_radius
  • target_detection_type_selection
  • pallet_det_min_points_close_to_first_point
  • pallet_det_2_max_dist_to_first_point
  • pallet_det_use_dynamic_search_ang
  • pallet_det_limit_initial_search_ang
  • target_detection_max_dist_to_symbolic_point

Unwanted cluster between legs

The unwanted cluster is a feature that tries to find object between the two-legged pallet on the first row. The main idea is to add cost to model that has something between its legs or to discard that model. On the picture down below is an example case where there are two small pallets next to each other and larger model (the green box) is detected instead. This has been created mainly to avoid detecting large two-legged models, if there are also smaller models. For example, if the picture down below would have large model (the green box) that would fit to the outer legs, but we want to detect only the smaller one this would be useful.

The parameter pallet_det_2_fitness_cost_percentage_for_unwanted_cluster defines if the fitness of that result is only decreased by some percentage value or if the model is discarded (negative value) when unwanted cluster is found between the legs.

The parameter pallet_det_2_min_points_to_create_unwanted_cluster define how many points is needed between the legs to create cluster. On the figure down below, the unwanted cluster is circled with red.

The parameter pallet_det_2_max_point_diff_for_unwanted_cluster define the point difference related to the side legs. The idea is to detect only similar size clusters as the outer legs.

Unwanted cluster example. The pallet has unwanted cluster (the red circle), between the front legs. Depending on the case, the result can be discarded or the fitness value can be decreased to add more weight to other results.

Target filtering

Target detection results are filtered, before they are given for route planner to calculate a docking path.

Target detection filtering parameters:

F,target_detection_results_dampening, (0.30 by default)

Dampening value for the results; min = 0.0, max = 1

Filters as a low pass filter, so bigger value means that location is updated faster, lower means more filtering is done

I, target_min_n_times_detected, (20 by default)

Minimum number of times target must be detected successfully before target is approved as found.

I, target_max_n_times_missing, (300 by default)

Maximum number of times last known location of target is given if no target detected

This means that, if the target was detected successfully target_min_n_times_detected -times, the algorithm still uses the last known location for route planning, even if the target is not detected in less than target_max_n_times_missing -cycles. If target is not detected for larger amount of cycles, it is considered lost.

Navitrol Monitor Pallet Detection form

Navitrol Pallet Detection form can be opened in Navitrol Monitor from the menu Navitrol -> Pallet Detection Form, when the Monitor is connected to an AGV. It can be used for testing and debugging the pallet detection and the docking procedure it should execute.

The pallet detection form in Navitrol Monitor

All the Navitrol parameters shown on the form can be changed to Navitrol params.txt by changing the value on the field manually and pressing the button 'Upload & Save parameters'. It saves the new parameters and reloads them into use. If the checkbox 'Save new params to file' is unchecked, parameters are only loaded into use for current run, but are not saved to the params.txt. 'Reload current parameters' can be then used to use again the parameters on the params.txt.

Short explanations for the fields shown on the form:

Driving to location with pallet det enabled

When the end location of the current driving order has the pallet detection enabled from Navithor Tools, this box turns green and shows 1. Otherwise it is red and 0 and the docking procedure will never start.

Pallet detection algorithm started

If pallet detection is activated in Navitrol parameters, the AGV is driving into a location with pallet detection enabled and we are close enough to the symbolic point (on default 6 meters), the AGV starts to search for the pallet and this box turns green and shows 1. The algorithm can be also always activated with Navitrol parameters, but that should be usually used only for debugging purposes.

Target found

This box turns green and shows 1, when the target is found.

Target detected n times

Shows how many times in a row the target is properly seen. There is a parameter to limit for how many times the target has to be first send, before the target is locked and docking can be started.

The parameter is shown on the right side: target_min_n_times_detected

Target missing n times

If the target is already locked (the target is seen enough, so that the limit above is fulfilled), the old location for the target is used even if the algorithm doesn't detect the pallet every calculation round. Only after the target is missing for too long time, the docking procedure is cancelled. The parameter for how many times the target can be missing is shown on the right side: target_max_n_times_missing

Distance from scanner to the target

Minimum distance from the scanner to the calculated stopping point for the target location to be updated. Scanners usually have more error in close ranges, so this parameter can be used to just use the last known location for the target, after it is closer than the distance specified in pallet_det_min_dist_from_scanner.

NOTE: If target_detection_type_selection is 3, pallet_det_2_min_dist_from_nav_point_to_traj_end is used instead for the same purpose.

Docking started

The box will stay red until all the 5 restrictions below are met and the docking can be started. After that the box will turn green.

Angle difference between symbolic point heading and angle from navigation point to the symbolic point

Parameter to change: docking_max_start_angle

Angle difference between symbolic point's and AGV's headings

Parameter to change: docking_max_start_heading

Angle difference between target angle and symbolic point's heading

Parameter to change: docking_max_target_angle

Distance error between symbolic point and the calculated target stopping point (stopping offset included)

Troubleshoot

This chapter introduces some issues that customer might face with pallet detection or docking and how to resolve them.

Pallet detection is not started when driving to symbolic point

When driving to Symbolic point, there is no green box on the Monitor or Client that indicates that pallet has been found. There might be several reasons, but here are the most common ones.

Tag missing

Problem: In this case, there is no warning or error, because the pallet detection or docking is not activated.

What to do: Check that the symbolic point has pallet detection tag on Tools.

Pallet detection tag for symbolic point on Tools.

Target detection not enabled

Problem: On this case, there is no error or warning, because the feature is not enabled. Even if there is the pallet detection tag.

What to do: Check that target_detection_type_selection has 2 or 3.

Closest point not found

Problem: The AGV stops with TARGET_APPROACH_ERROR and on the log just before this error there is a line "Pallet detection ERROR: Closest point for lin mod" or "Pallet det: Closest point for legs not found."

What to do:

  • If pallet_det_use_dynamic_search_ang is set to 1, check that pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add has large enough value. It is used to calculate the search area for the closest point.

Dynamic search angle calculation for two-legged and three-legged pallet. It is calculated based on distance to target and model width + pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add parameter.

  • If pallet_det_use_dynamic_search_ang is set to 0, check that pallet_det_limit_initial_search_ang has large enough search angle.

  • Check that pallet_det_min_points_close_to_first_point has smaller value than scanner points in the smallest cluster on the front row.

The middle leg has only two scanner points and the value for the pallet_det_min_points_close_to_first_point parameter was 2. Meaning that there should be total of three points in a distance of pallet_det_2_max_dist_to_first_point.

Scanner points are discarded

Problem: If target_detection_type_selection is 3, the parameter pallet_det_min_dist_from_scanner is used to discard scanner points that are too close to the scanner. One indication for this case is on the log print where the pallet detection is started. If there is this kind of log print: "Starting pallet det v2 algorithm, pointsnbr 0", it means that there are no scanner points for the algorithm.

What to do: Set this pallet_det_min_dist_from_scanner parameter to small value, so it doesn't discard points in too far distance.

Pallet model parameters are incorrect

Problem: The model is not found, because the width and length are incorrect or the number of legs is not correct. In many cases it is better to verify the actual leg size and pallet width from Monitor (using the Measurement tape button on the top bar), because the sizes depend on the scanner resolution and the distance. The longer the distance to the target, the less accurate scanner data is, because of the scanner resolution.

The pallet_model_width_y parameters is always the front side where the AGV is approaching to the pallet and pallet_model_length_x is the side. For example, when picking the EUR-pallet from normal direction, the width is 0.8 m and the length is 1.2 m. If this same EUR-pallet can be picked up from the side (approaching from the 1.2 m side), then there has to be other model that defines its dimensions. Then that model width is 1.2 m and length is 0.8 m. Also, the leg dimensions have to be set accordingly.

Here the pallet dimensions are same, but there has to be different model based on approaching direction.

What to do: Check Chapter 5 to verify that the model parameters are okay in general.

Pallet detected in wrong location

Pallet is too much on the side

Problem: There are several pallets next to each other. The pallet is found too much to the side, based on the pallet next to the desired one.

Pallet detected on side. The desired result is the two-legged one close to the symbolic point.

What to do: Decrease pallet_det_2_initial_search_model_width_add to decrease the search area or decrease pallet_det_2_max_dist_to_route to discard pallets that are found too far from the route.

Pallet angle changes during drive

Problem: AGV drives towards the pallet, but the target angle changes suddenly. This causes AGV to turn off the route, because it will try to reach the changed target. On the three example pictures down below, the scanner sees first the stringer board, then some part between the legs and the stringer board and finally the legs of the pallet. The main problem here is that the scanner is lifted during the docking procedure. In general, during the pallet detection, the scanner should only see points that can be detected as a pallet (for example not the floor in front of the pallet).

Pallet is seen from the stringer board. Docking is not started yet.

Pallet is seen a bit below the stringer board or the scanner sees something below the pallet. This part of the pallet is not very reasonable for the detection and causes wrong angle. The docking is started based on this detection.

Pallet is seen from the legs.

What to do:

  • Change the docking_start_distance so the docking starts only after the pallet is detected correctly.

  • Check tilt of the scanner. The scanner should see only the legs or the stringer board of the pallet.

  • Decrease pallet_det_2_max_pallet_angle_error to discard pallets that have too high angle error.
